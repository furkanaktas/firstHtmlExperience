GRACE MURRAY HOPPER

Grace Hopper was an american scientist,computer scientist,mathematician and rear admiral.She was a scientist who was first programmer of “Hardvard Mark 1” computer and devolopped first compiler for computer programming languages.She was one of the first users of cleaning concept of the program error known as “debugging”. Also she was one of the developers of “COBOL” which was one of the first modern programming languages.

Early Life And Education

Hopper was born on December 9 in 1906 in New York City.She was the eldest among three siblings.Her parents were Dutch and Scottish descent.Alexander Wilson Russel who was her grandfather was an Admiral in US Navy and he had made war in the Battle of Mobile Bay during the Civil War.Grace Hopper studied math and physics at Vassar Collage.In 1928 after graduating from Vassar,she kept going to Yale University in 1930.She received a master’s degree in mathematics in there.She married with Vincent Foster Hopper at same years.She used same name even after that couples got divorced in 1945.She began teaching at Vassar while also going on to study at Yale.She became one of the first few woman to earn such a degree by earning a Ph.D. in mathematics in 1934 at Yale.

Career

World War II

Hopper received permisson from Vassar in 1943 during the World War II to attend the United States Navy Reserve to be one of many women volunteer to serve in WAVES(Women Accepted for Volunteer Emergency Service) and she tained mariteme education in there.She graduated first in her class in 1944.After graduation,she began computing projects at Harvard University as a lieutenant.She worked in “Mark I” programming languaage team which led by presidential Howard H. Aiken and she published articles about Mark I,II,III with Aiken.She worked in Harvard Computation Labs until 1949.

Univac

Hopper started working at Eckert–Mauchly Computer Corporation as a mathematician and joined the team devoloping the UNIVAC I .At the beginning of 1950’s,the company was taken over by the Remington Rand corporation.Meanwhile,she completed her complier work. The compiler was known as the A compiler and its first version was A-0.In 1954,Hopper was company's first director of automatic programming and her department created some of the first compiler-based programming languages such as MATH-MATIC and FLOW-MATIC.

Cobol

Hopper supported that programming languages should be closer to the English language instead of a machine language. Towards the end of 1950s,she started working on COBOL which was one of the first modern programming languages with computer experts.She led to develop FORTRAN and COBOL standards and tested these standards.In 1980s,these tests were accepted by ISO (International Organization for Standardization).She was promoted to major-general because of her successes and was retired in 1986.She got outstanding service awards at ceremony which was arranged in Boston.She worked in Digital Equipment company until rest of her life and she closed her eyes to life on january 1 in 1992.
